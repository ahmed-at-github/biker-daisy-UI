Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?
Safety is paramount! Always wear proper protective gear, follow traffic laws, and maintain your motorcycle regularly. Get proper training and never ride beyond your skill level. Always be visible and predictable on the road.
How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?
Replace your helmet if it's over 5 years old, has visible cracks, loose padding, worn straps, or has been in any accident. Even minor impacts can compromise the helmet's protective capabilities.
My bike has been in storage. Is it safe to ride?
Before riding, check tire pressure and condition, test brakes, inspect fluid levels, ensure the chain is properly lubricated, and verify all lights work. A professional inspection is recommended for extended storage periods.
What rules should I follow when riding my bike?
Always wear a DOT-approved helmet and protective gear, use proper hand signals, ride with traffic flow, maintain a safe following distance, stay alert and avoid distractions, and never ride under the influence.
